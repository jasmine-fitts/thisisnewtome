Robyn "Rihanna" Fenty

BIO

Born in Saint Michael, Barbados, Robyn Rihanna Fenty is a singer, songwriter, and actress. In 2003, Rihanna signed her first recording contract with Def Jam Recordings. Rihanna quickly rose to fame with the release of her debut album Music of the Sun and its followup A Girl Like Me, whose successful singles included Pon de Replay, SOS, and Unfaithful.

In 2007, Rihanna rebranded her image and reinvented her music. Her lead single “Umbrella” earned her her first Grammy Award. Since the “Good Girl Gone Bad"(2007) era, Rihanna released four consecutive platinum studio albums, one of which Unapologetic, which she a Grammy Award for Best Urban Contemporary Album in 2014. Rihanna’s eighth studio album, Anti, reached number one on the Billboard 200 and became the most streamed albums of the year.

With 230 million records sold worldwide, Rihanna is one of the best-selling music artist of all time. She is the youngest solo artist to earn fourteen number-one singles on the Billboard Hot 200 and has a total of thirty one top-ten singles. She’s also Spotify’s most streamed female artist of all time. In 2012, Forbes ranked her the fourth most powerful celebrity, while Time included her on the annual list of the most influential people in the world.
